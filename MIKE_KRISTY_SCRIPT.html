Mike & Kristy — Conversation Script

Mike Stevenson → Kristy Singal, Trustee Solutions (TSI) · May 2026
The situation: Mike has a 20-year relationship with Kristy. He's not introducing software — he's introducing people he's partnered with and vouching for personally. The frame is: "I want you to meet these guys." The product comes second. The goal is a conversation, not a demo.
The one goal: Get Kristy to agree to meet Joe and Carmen — or take a call with them. Mike's credibility is the door opener. He's saying: I've partnered with these people, I believe in what they've built, and I think it's worth your time to know them.

The Opening

Mike
Kristy — I wanted to reach out because I've partnered with some people I think you should meet. Joe and Carmen Bartilotta — they've built something in the financial intelligence space that I genuinely think could benefit trustees, and I mean that in a real way, not just as a pitch. I've been using it on my own cases. It reads bank statements the way a forensic accountant would — surfaces things you'd miss in a normal review. I'm involved because I believe in it, and you're someone I respect enough to bring it to directly. Would you be open to a conversation with them? Even just a quick call — I think you'd find it worth your time.
Mike is vouching for Joe and Carmen as partners first, the software second. That's the key shift. Let her respond before adding anything.

If She Asks Questions

Kristy: "What exactly does it do?"
You upload the debtor's bank statements and it spits back a report in about five minutes — risk score, every flagged transaction, the questions to ask at the 341. It doesn't replace your CMS. It just tells you what's hiding in the statements that a normal review misses. That's the part nobody's built yet.
Kristy: "How does it fit with what we do at TSI?"
That's exactly why I'm calling you first. TSI is the operations and admin layer for trustees. This is the intelligence layer on top of the statements. Different problem — I actually think it's more complementary than anything else. Joe can speak to that better than I can, but that's why I wanted you to hear about it before someone else did.
Kristy: "Who are these people?"
Joe and Carmen Bartilotta — Michigan-based. Joe's the builder, Carmen's his partner in the business. They've been deep in financial intelligence software for years. I came in as a founding partner because after I saw what they built and spent time with them, I thought it was real. Good people. Smart. They're not looking for a room full of investors — they want to talk to people in the trustee world who can tell them whether this is as useful as I think it is.
Kristy: "Is this something you want me to invest in?"
No — I'm not asking you for anything like that. I just think you should know them. If there's something here that fits with what you're doing at TSI or helps the trustees you work with, great. If not, no harm done. Just a conversation.
Kristy: "I'm pretty busy right now."
I hear you. It's fifteen minutes, and I genuinely think it's worth it. How does next week look — even just a quick screen-share? I can be on it too if that helps.

The Close

Mike
I'll connect you. Joe and Carmen are good people — you'll like them. It'll be a real conversation, not a pitch. They'll show you what it does and you can tell them what you think from the trustee side. That feedback means something to them. Can I make the intro?
Get the yes, then make the intro. Mike's job is done — Joe and Carmen take it from there.

What Not To Do

Don't: explain the full product · quote pricing · mention competitors · use words like "platform," "AI," or "machine learning" · make it longer than it needs to be. Twenty years of trust is the pitch. Use it.
