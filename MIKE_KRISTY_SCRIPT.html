Mike & Kristy — Conversation Script

Mike Stevenson → Kristy Singal, Trustee Solutions (TSI) · May 2026
The situation: Mike has a 20-year relationship with Kristy. This is not a sales call — it's a trusted colleague sharing something useful. The only goal of this conversation is to get Kristy to say yes to a 15-minute look with Joe. Mike doesn't need to explain the product. He just needs to open the door.
The one goal: Get Kristy to agree to a short call or screen-share with Joe so he can show her what the tool actually does. That's it. Don't pitch. Don't explain. Just get the meeting.

The Opening

Mike
Kristy — I'm not calling to sell you anything, I promise. You know me better than that. But I've been using something the last few weeks on my cases and I keep thinking you should see it before it goes wider. It's a software tool that reads bank statements the way a forensic accountant would — flags the things you'd miss in a normal review. Crypto, hidden transfers, structured deposits. I've caught things on it I wouldn't have found otherwise. I think it fits right alongside what you're doing at TSI. Would you give Joe fifteen minutes to show you what it does?
That's the whole opener. Warm, plain, peer-to-peer. Let her respond before adding anything.

If She Asks Questions

Kristy: "What exactly does it do?"
You upload the debtor's bank statements and it spits back a report in about five minutes — risk score, every flagged transaction, the questions to ask at the 341. It doesn't replace your CMS. It just tells you what's hiding in the statements that a normal review misses. That's the part nobody's built yet.
Kristy: "How does it fit with what we do at TSI?"
That's exactly why I'm calling you first. TSI is the operations and admin layer for trustees. This is the intelligence layer on top of the statements. Different problem — I actually think it's more complementary than anything else. Joe can speak to that better than I can, but that's why I wanted you to hear about it before someone else did.
Kristy: "Who built it?"
Joe Bartilotta — Michigan-based, been building financial intelligence software for years. I came in as a founding partner because I think it's real. It's not a startup pitch, it's a working product. I've been running it on my own cases.
Kristy: "Is this something you want me to invest in?"
No — not at all. I'm not asking you for anything. I just think you should see it. If it's useful for TSI or for the trustees you work with, great. If it's not the right fit, no harm done. Fifteen minutes with Joe, that's it.
Kristy: "I'm pretty busy right now."
I hear you. It's fifteen minutes, and I genuinely think it's worth it. How does next week look — even just a quick screen-share? I can be on it too if that helps.

The Close

Mike
I'll have Joe reach out to set it up. He'll keep it short — show you what it does on a sample case, let you see the output, and you can tell him what you think. No pressure either way. Sound good?
Get the yes, then hand it to Joe. Mike's job is done.

What Not To Do

Don't: explain the full product · quote pricing · mention competitors · use words like "platform," "AI," or "machine learning" · make it longer than it needs to be. Twenty years of trust is the pitch. Use it.
