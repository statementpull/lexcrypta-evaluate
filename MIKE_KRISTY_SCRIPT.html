Mike & Kristy — Conversation Script

Mike Stevenson → Kristy Singal, Trustee Solutions (TSI) · May 2026
The situation: Mike has a 20-year relationship with Kristy. He's not introducing software — he's introducing people he's taken a position with. He put skin in the game. That changes everything. The frame is: "I want you to meet these guys — and I'm in with them." The product comes second. The goal is a conversation, not a demo.
The one goal: Get Kristy to agree to meet Joe and Carmen. Mike's credibility — and the fact that he took a founding position — is the door opener. This isn't a cold referral. He believed in it enough to be part of it.

The Opening

Mike
Kristy — I wanted to reach out because I've taken a position with some people I think you should meet. Joe and Carmen Bartilotta — they've built something in the financial intelligence space that I believe in enough that I came on as a founding partner. I'm not just recommending it from the outside, I'm in it with them. They've built a tool that reads bank statements the way a forensic accountant would — surfaces the things that a normal review misses. I've been running it on my own cases and it's real. They're starting to get in front of some other groups now and I wanted you amongst the first — given what you do at TSI, I think it belongs in that conversation early. Would you be open to a quick call with them? I'd like to make the intro.
The urgency is soft — "starting to get in front of other groups" — not a hard close. It's respectful. Let her respond before adding anything.

If She Asks Questions

Kristy: "What exactly does it do?"
It starts with bank statements — you upload them and in about five minutes you get a report back. Risk score, every flagged transaction, the questions to bring to the 341. Crypto transfers, structured deposits, wire anomalies — the stuff that's buried in the noise. That's the core tool and it works right now. But the real value is the next layer — they've built something called Evaluate that's designed specifically for deeper financial analysis. Think M&A due diligence, complex cases, situations where you need a full forensic pass on a set of financials, not just statements. That's where it gets serious. And there's more in development beyond that — Joe can walk you through where it's heading. The bank statements tool is the door in. Evaluate is what made me take a position.
Kristy: "How does it fit with what we do at TSI?"
That's exactly why I'm calling you first. TSI is the operations and admin layer for trustees. This is the intelligence layer on top of the statements. Different problem — I actually think it's more complementary than anything else. Joe can speak to that better than I can, but that's why I wanted you to hear about it before someone else did.
Kristy: "Who are these people?"
Joe and Carmen Bartilotta — Michigan-based. Joe's the builder, Carmen's his partner in the business. I took a founding position because after I saw what they built and spent time with them, I thought it was worth being part of. That's not something I do lightly. Good people, serious about what they're doing. They're not trying to fill a room — they want to talk to people in the trustee world who can actually tell them if this is as useful as I think it is.
Kristy: "Is this something you want me to invest in?"
No — I'm not asking you for anything like that. I just think you should know them. If there's something here that fits with what you're doing at TSI or helps the trustees you work with, great. If not, no harm done. Just a conversation.
Kristy: "I'm pretty busy right now."
I hear you — and I wouldn't push if I didn't think it mattered. They're starting to move now and I'd rather you were at the front of that than catch it later through someone else. Fifteen minutes, next week. I'll be on the call too. What day works?

The Close

Mike
I'll make the intro today. Joe and Carmen are good people — you'll like them. It won't be a pitch, it'll be a real conversation. They'll start with the bank statements tool so you can see what it does on an actual case, then walk you through Evaluate — that's the layer that made me get involved. And they'll give you a feel for where it's heading beyond that. You'll have a full picture. I just wanted you in that room early. Can I send the intro now?
Get the yes, then make the intro same day. Mike's job is done — Joe and Carmen take it from there.

What Not To Do

Don't: explain the full product · quote pricing · mention competitors · use words like "platform," "AI," or "machine learning" · make it longer than it needs to be. Twenty years of trust is the pitch. Use it.
